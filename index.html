My Application to the FAC Spring 2020 Cohort.

Hello! My name is Jake Powis & iv'e recently entered start up land after leaving a nearly 10 year career in banking (most recently 3 years at Silicon Valley Bank) and am looking to join the spring cohort to get my (very limited) coding skills up to speed in order to support my ambition of pivoting my career into working with early stage tech companies - with the ultimate aspiration of founding & running a London based Fintech business!


My goal from this programme and thereafter is therefore to develop strong coding skills, integrate myself into the developer community, build out my tech network, find a cool start up company to work with (most lilkey in fintech), and then eventually start building out my own start-up ideas (see FAC section for more details). I'm also excited about the opportunity to contribute to the programme in the future and help support the education of all future cohorts to come.

Thank you for taking the time to read my application & look at my first ever website!

My Journey

pre-2009

I graduated from the university of Sussex in Brighton in 2009 with a 1st class hons degree in Economics (with some electives in Japanese language).

JAPAN
2009-2010

After graduation, i traveled to Japan (Nagoya) for a year to teach English in a conversational English school (teaching all levles, from kids to business men) and to better improve my Japanese, including spending some time living with a Japanese family & attending an intensive Japanese language school in Okazaki.

HSBC
2010-2016

I returned home and got accepted to the HSBC Graduate Scheme, and once completed i become a junior Associate in the Corporate Bank, spending c.3 years supporting the overseas growth of International focused businesses in Kent, and then later moved to Bristol and spent c.3 years providing funding for Private Equity in their acquisitions of business based across the west of England & Wales.

Silicon Valley Bank
2016-2019

Being interested in Technology, i made the move to Silicon Valley Bank in London, a tech & innovation focused bank that specialise in lending to Start-ups and Venture Capital backed companies. During my 3 years at SVB i moved from Senior Associate to Senior Vice President, having the opportunity to work with and support some really greak UK & European based tech companies, and was even able to spend time in San Fran, San Diego, Palo Alto, Boston & Chicago to really understand the startup/tech culture in the US.

Coder / Founder

After a rewarding period at SVB working with great tech companies, the itch to start my own enterprise became too strong and in July'19 i left my role in order to pursue the idea of creating my own start up and have since been working on a number of projects. I have now come to the retaliation that developing my own coding skills would vastly aid my journey, and in assessing my options came across FAC which appears to be a great fit for me as an aspiring Tech-Founder.

Why FAC

  • Peer Learning
    • FAC offers the ability to learn from your peers in a group environment, something which is incredibly exciting and i believe being in a group full of bright, diverse and dedicated minds will not only be a tremendously effective and motivating way to not only learn coding skills, but also develop stronger skills in teamwork, cooperation and communication.
    • I also love the Social Enterprise nature of FAC which creates a virtuous circle of free education for those who most need it and are willing to work hard for it. I am looking forward to giving back to the programme in the future, sharing what I've learnt in a virtuous cycle of free education.
    • FAC also embodies diversity, perhaps something lacking in my previous industry (banking) - this programme creates an environment of diverse thought and perspective, and i am therefore excited to learn from my teachers and fellow students, all of which from a variety of nationalities, experiences, and backgrounds.
  • Peer Teaching - One of the big draws of the programme is the ability to get back in the class room and pass on my learnings to future intakes. Teaching is something i really enjoy and has played a big role in all of my jobs, experiences include:
    • One year of full time teaching of conversational English at a Language School in Japan ;
    • Graduate education at Silicon Valley Bank (designing & delivering class room learning to new graduates & interns as part of their development). Was also heavily involved in all new grad and intern recruitment interviews and assessment centres;
    • Led on-campus grad employment skills workshops while at HSBC across various London universities, as well as attended all the major grad recruitment fairs representing HSBC.
    • Other experience includes coaching new rowers at my old rowing club in Bristol, tutoring Economics and Statistics at university, and ongoing informal mentorship for a number of mentees.
