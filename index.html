Jake Powis. FAC Spring 2020 Application:

Hello! My name is Jake Powis & iv'e recently entered start up land after leaving a nearly 10 year career in banking (most recently 3 years at Silicon Valley Bank) and am looking to join the spring cohort to get my (very limited) coding skills up to speed in order to support my ambition of pivoting my career into working with early stage tech companies - with the ultimate aspiration of founding & running a London based fintech or edtech business.

A key driver behind this ambition is move into a positon where i'm creating real products with a true positive social impact, helping those truly in need of financial services, education or funding, where the current system doesnt feel like it best serves the interests of everyone equally. I'm also excited about the opportunity to intergrate myself with the FAC developer community, share ideas, learn loads, and then be in a position to contribute back to the programme in the future to help support the education of all future cohorts to come.

Thank you for taking the time to read my application & look at my first ever website!

Why FAC

  • Peer Learning
    • FAC offers the ability to learn from your peers in a group environment, something which is incredibly exciting and i believe being in a group full of bright, diverse and dedicated minds will not only be a tremendously effective and motivating way to not only learn coding skills, but also to develop stronger skills in teamwork, cooperation and communication.
    • I also love the Social Enterprise nature of FAC which creates a virtuous circle of free education for those who most need it and are willing to work hard for it. As someone from a modest background who at times felt like they had to fight their way into an industry dominated by wealth and privilege, nothing makes me more passionate than being able to support equality of opportunity for all, regardless of background.
    • FAC also embodies diversity, perhaps something lacking in my previous industry - this programme creates an environment of diverse thought and perspective, and i am therefore excited to learn from my teachers and fellow students, all of which from a variety of nationalities, experiences, and backgrounds.
  • Peer Teaching - One of the big draws of the programme is the ability to get back in the class room and pass on my learnings to future intakes. Teaching is something i really enjoy and has played a big role in all of my jobs, experiences include:
    • One year of full time teaching of conversational English at a Language School in Japan ;
    • Graduate education at Silicon Valley Bank (designing & delivering class room learning to new graduates & interns as part of their 1st year curriculum). I was also heavily involved in new grad and intern recruitment interviews and assessment centers at both HSBC & SVB, as well as facilitating many on-campus grad employment and finance skills workshops across various Universities and High schools as HSBC community outreach.
    • Other experience includes coaching new rowers at my old rowing club in Bristol, tutoring Economics and Statistics at university, and ongoing informal mentorship for a number of mentees of various backgrounds.
  • To build something that matters. As part of my transition out of the world of banking, i'm excited to work with new start ups on their ideas, with the ambition to work with companies that offer a positive social impact and disrupt traditional finance, with the aim of one day building something of my own once i have the skills and experience. Current ideas for my own start-up projects include:
    Tools to support equality of access to Finance jobs

    I'm passionate about giving people equal opportunity and access to career opportunities, and while improving, unfortunately the current state of the finance industry often doesn't offer this (with connections and education often being the definitive factors in recruitment). Using my experience in recruiting and education grads, it should be possible to develop online education tools and resources to provide a free finance career-focused financial education (like free code camp, but for Finance) that would expand opportunity to entry level positions in finance and provide and more even playing field for those without higher educations to gain knowledge on practical financing concepts and techniques for free, that could help them get a foot on the ladder.
    Online tools to support early stage businesses

    There is often a disparity of information and knowledge in early stage businesses when raising finance or equity, leading to more lender/equity favored outcomes in these negotiations. Building out a public access library of tools and information based on what Financial Institutions and Equity providers use could help level the playing field hugely benefit companies when making these decisions (examples include Tools such as bespoke Debt capacity calculator & cashflow forecasters that utilize scrapping of financial statements for personalized results, or providing access to standard legal document templates & explanations of key areas and there practical impact on the business post deal). Providing these kind of tools and education to companies in a user friendly way would effectively help them to safely borrow or raise money and allow them to get the best deal in any funding round.
    Disruptive Fintech

    To create or work with companies providing new avenues to finance and banking products to those with traditionally limited access. London is a hub of FinTech companies, and I'd be excited to be able to use my experience and knowledge of traditional finance, alongside my coding and development skills in order to support more tech-driven and socially responsible approaches to providing financing.

    I'm also extremely interested in Edtech given my experience and interest in education, and would love to work with a tech company that supports open access to education, as i feel strongly that this is one of the strongest drivers to increasing social equality.

    Thank you for taking the time to consider my application, and i hope to meet you all in person soon!
