My Application to the FAC Spring 2020 Cohort

Hello! My name is Jake Powis & iv'e recently entered start up land after a nearly 10 year career in banking (most recently 3 years at Silicon Valley Bank) and am looking to join the spring cohort to get my (very limited) coding skills up to speed in order to support my aspirations of pivoting into fintech and hopefully one day founding a London based fintech company!


My goal from this programme is therefore to develop strong coding skills, integrate myself into the developer community, start building out my start-up ideas (see FAC section for more details!), and hopefully then be in a position to give back to the community through the peer teaching element of this pro gramme.


Thank you for taking the time to read my application & look at my first ever website!

Why FAC

  • Peer Learning
    • FAC offers the ability to learn from your peers in a group environment, something which is incredibly exciting and i believe being in a group full of bright, diverse and dedicated minds will not only be a tremendously effective and motivating way to not only learn coding skills, but also develop stronger skills in teamwork, cooperation and communication.
    • I also love the Social Enterprise nature of FAC which creates a virtuous circle of free education for those who most need it and are willing to work hard for it. I am looking forward to giving back to the programme in the future.
    • FAC also embodies diversity, perhaps something lacking in my previous industry (banking) - this programme creates an environment of diverse thought and perspective, and i am therefore excited to learn from my teachers and fellow students, all of which from a variety of nationalities, experiences, and backgrounds.
  • Peer Teaching - One of the big draws of the programme is the ability to get back in the class room and pass on my learnings to future intakes. Teaching is something i really enjoy and has played a big role in all of my jobs, experiences include:
    • One year of full time teaching in conversational English in Japan at a Language School;
    • Graduate education at Silicon Valley Bank(including 6 hour course designed & delivered by myself to all new grads). Was also heavily involved in all new grad and intern recruitment interviews and assessment centres;
    • Delivered on campus grad employment educational workshops while at HSBC across various London univerisites, as well as attended all the major grad recruitment fairs representing HSBC.
    • Other experience includes coaching new rowers at my old rowing club in Bristol, tutoring Economics and Statistics at university, and ongoing informal mentorship for 4 mentees.
